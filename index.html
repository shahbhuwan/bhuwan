Climate-Resilient Conservation Framework

Future-proofing Iowa's soil and water conservation

The Problem: Conservation practice designs rely on historical climate data that no longer represents future conditions under climate change.
My Solution: Developed a scalable framework integrating high-resolution, bias-corrected climate projections into WEPP erosion modeling. Tested across 21 watersheds with scenarios including contouring and vegetative buffers.
The Impact: Presented at the Iowa Water Conference. Supports Iowa's nutrient reduction goals and long-term conservation planning under projected extremes.

SAR Soil Moisture Retrieval

Seeing through crops with physics-constrained ML

The Problem: C-band SAR satellites can't accurately measure soil moisture under dense crop canopies ‚Äî a critical "blind spot" across the entire Corn Belt.
My Solution: Built a physics-constrained machine learning framework that fuses L-band (structural penetration) with C-band (surface sensitivity) SAR data for continental-scale soil moisture retrieval.

NRCS AWM Climate Integration

Stress-testing federal infrastructure tools with future climate

The Problem: The NRCS Animal Waste Management tool only uses historical climate, making it blind to increasing extreme storm risks under climate change.
My Solution: Developed bias-corrected, downscaled CMIP6 climate projection inputs compatible with the AWM tool, enabling it to simulate under future climate scenarios.
The Impact: Revealed ~17% increases in extreme storm intensity. Directly supports federal agency decision-making for manure storage design and environmental compliance.
